Teach a Workshop

Share your skills and knowledge with the MakeHaven community.

We're thrilled that you're interested in sharing your skills! Below is all the information you'll need to plan and submit a successful workshop. If you are new to teaching, we can assist with scheduling a test-run of the class.

Workshop Requirements & Guidelines

Make & Take

Design a project that participants can complete and take with them at the end of the session. Achievable within the timeframe.

50% Hands-On Activity

At least half of the workshop time should be dedicated to practical, hands-on experience using tools or software.

Pamphlet Summary

Create a concise pamphlet summarizing key takeaways, techniques, and resources as a reference guide.

Instructional Slideshow

Prepare a slideshow or visual aid that walks participants through the main points of the workshop.

Materials on GDrive

All materials used (slides, pamphlets, instructions) must be saved and organized on the MakeHaven GDrive.

What Happens Next

Once the submission form is complete and we confirm dates, we will send you a copy of the instructor agreement and get you set up on our payroll system.

The event will be made live on the calendar. We require a minimum one month lead from live on the calendar to the event start, to give sufficient time for sign-ups.

If you're not a member, you'll be set up in the MakeHaven system as an instructor. You will come in for an orientation and receive an ID for workshop-related access.

Ticket Price Formula
(Instructor Pay + MakeHaven Costs) / Minimum Attendees + Supply Cost

This gives us the MakeHaven member ticket cost. Members get 15% off the public prices. Payment is processed each Monday for workshops that occurred the previous week.
