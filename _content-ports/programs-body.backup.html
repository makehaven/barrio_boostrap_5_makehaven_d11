We equip people with tools to design, invent, and create through our program areas. Explore our offerings below to see how you can get involved.

Exploration & Empowerment

These programs are designed to provide access to tools, knowledge, and a creative community, fostering personal growth and collaborative projects.

  • Community Workshop: 24/7 member access to our woodshop, textile lab, prototyping equipment, digital fabrication tools, and machine shop.
  • Fabrication Education Mentorship: Learn from over 20 volunteer experts who provide tool training, safety checks, and project guidance.
  • Public Meetups: Join public groups centered around shared interests to learn and collaborate.
  • University & VA Hospital Partnerships: We host university classes and veterans' groups, integrating fabrication with education and therapeutic goals.
  • First Robotics: Serving as the build space for a local high school competitive robotics team.
  • Precious Plastic Initiative: Join our community effort to recycle plastic into new and useful creations.
  • Upcoming Events & Workshops: View all ticketed sessions to gain new experiences and learn new skills.
  • Events Calendar: See what's happening at MakeHaven at a glance.
  • Private Classes: Schedule a custom class for your group, tailored to your interests and skill level.
  • Team-Building Workshops: Foster collaboration and creativity with our unique hands-on workshops for organizations.
  • HAM Radio License: Get the training you need to earn your Level 1 Technician Class License in amateur radio.

Employment Skills & Certification

Our certificate and pre-apprenticeship programs are designed to build tangible skills for career advancement in fabrication and the trades.

  • GEMS: MakeHaven Intro Program: A guided exploration of the tools, technology, and skills available in our makerspace.
  • GEMS Alumni Program: Continue your maker journey with advanced projects and community support after completing the intro program.
  • Foundations of Fabrication: A comprehensive certificate program covering the essentials of making.
  • Pathway to Trades Pre-Apprentice Program: Gain foundational skills and connections for a successful career in the skilled trades.
  • WCA Sawblade Certification: Earn a valuable credential from the Woodworking Career Alliance.
  • Art of Sewing Skills Program: A partnership with the New Haven School District.

Business Incubation

We provide the resources, mentorship, and space for entrepreneurs and small ventures to grow and succeed.

  • Workspace Rental: Low-cost, month-to-month co-working spaces.
  • Startup Coaching: Get guidance from volunteers trained by the Small Business Administration.
  • Prototype-to-Product Pipeline: Access resources to take your idea from concept to production.
  • Educational Seminars: Attend sessions on intellectual property, marketing, design for manufacturing, and more.
  • Networking Events: Connect with a broad network of entrepreneurs, fabricators, and support services.
